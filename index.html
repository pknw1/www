When approaching any issue, it's essential to understand all the variables that could come into play

  • Identify and Inventory processes, inputs, actions, outputs 
  • Identify business ownership and Subject Matter Experts to evaluate business value and associated risk
  • Using first principle analysis, break the process into smaller and smaller actions
  • Continually evaluate and ensure ownership, imperative and performance as to whether the current process is optimal

Leadership in any environment is essential to ensure focus and cohesion; the system wide view that drives no only engineers, but key-stakeholders, to bring out the best in them and their ability; Technical leadership can be driving aspiration within the team; the aspiration to approach a new technology; the aspiration to adopt new styles of working and ultimately the aspiration to lead others

  • Lead by example by showing both technical ability but also humility and honesty in approaching new territory
  • Lead by providing alternative view points such as consideration for cost, imperative
  • Lead by working in the team and coaching positive behaviors
  • Lead by assuming overall responsibility for any problems, while ensuring accolades go the engineers

Innovation can be valid in everything we do, both at work or when approaching any task

  • How are we currently or aiming to solve an issue
  • Is there community consensus around this or other solutions
  • Look at community solutions and containers that solve current issues
  • Can we identify other solutions?
  • Can we improve on others work?

Every member of a business should always be looking to gather and present their knowledge to their peers, the company or the community where it is deemed to have value to either improve support capability, enhance understanding, reduce risk, improve costs or to make the day to day lives of those around us simpler and more productive 

  • Education could be simply documenting a process to share
  • Businesses should have internal systems that (content depending) can be accessed by all 
  • Materials should be grouped or tagged so that cross-discipline and department we can share our processes and the way we work
  • We should educate out at scale should we determine that we can improve the business or the process by bringing in more users 

Engineering a solution depends on many factors for success, but the most critical is often simplicity

  • Broken into stages can we describe the steps for each stage in general non-tech specific terms
  • Can we communicate the solution and proposal to technical and non technical peers
  • Does our proposal have any code/elements already available that we can re-use?
  • Does our proposal fit with the skills and technology direction of the company?
  • Have we evaluated services or systems that we already have access or licence for to use before evaluating other solutions?
  • Have we discussed risk?
  • Have we understood development, testing and continuous delivery mechanisms for our work>
  • Have we defined user cases with stakeholders and ensured a definite definition of done is described and agreed

Not only DevOps and continuous methods require feedback - we should be looking to accept and nurture feedback at every level either looking to state that "the component or service acts in this way due to constraint X or Y" - but also by asking for detailed comment

  • Understanding how services or components work to be able to deliver an answer to the end users, or by looking it up in internal KB
  • Accounting for how recently the component was evaluated and how frequently comments for this come to the support or engineering teams
  • Tease out as much detail as possible to determine the root cause of repetitive issues - such as working "the system is slow" to a definite cause either by working side-by-side with users to visualize it, and/or using system metrics, logs and traps to determine possible discussion points with peers
